## Design Context

### Users

Individuals and families booking premium private transfers in Switzerland and the Alps — ski resort runs, airport pickups, hotel-to-hotel city rides. They browse on mobile while planning holidays and on desktop when comparing options. The booking decision has emotional weight: this is part of a special trip.

### Brand Personality

**Silent. Precise. Alpine.**
Like a Swiss watchmaker — no excess, no noise, everything intentional. Confidence through restraint.

### Aesthetic Direction

Dark cinematic with warm gold accents. Deep charcoal backgrounds (#0a0a0a), warm amber/gold (#d4a574 primary, #d4a843 bright accent), cream text (#f5f1e8). Bodoni Moda for display (elegant serif with high contrast strokes), Manrope for body (clean humanist sans).

NOT: cyan-on-dark tech vibes, purple gradients, neon glows, glassmorphism noise, or the generic "luxury travel" tropes (full-bleed car photos with white overlay and a cursive font).

Anti-patterns to never use:

- Gradient text (explicitly banned)
- Side-stripe border accents on cards
- Identical card grids with icon + heading + text repeated
- Blue/cool color ambient glows (wrong brand hue)
- Commented-out code left in JSX

### Design Principles

1. **Restraint earns trust** — whitespace and silence feel expensive. Don't fill every corner.
2. **Gold is rare** — amber/gold accents work because they're used sparingly. Every amber element should feel intentional.
3. **Hierarchy through weight, not gradient** — display font at high contrast creates hierarchy; gradient text is lazy.
4. **Touch targets matter** — this is a mobile-first booking product. Every tap target ≥ 44px.
5. **Consistent vocabulary** — charcoal backgrounds, cream text, amber accents. Every page follows the same token system.
